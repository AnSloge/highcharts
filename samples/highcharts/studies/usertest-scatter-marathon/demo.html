Scatter plot trends example - marathon dataset

Data source: data.world.

Here we are user testing a concept where we simplify scatter plots into trend lines. Below are five charts with different trend line detail. The charts all display the same data, major marathon winning times throughout history, with two plots - one for the male competition class and one for the female competition class.

We see how the winning times overall improve for males and females over time. Male marathon times start in 1897 and show a gradual linear decrease, with some variation. Female marathon times start later - in 1966 - and show a sharper non-linear decrease, which looks like a half of the letter U. After 1980 more marathons are run by both males and females.

Sonification information

The trend lines can be sonified - played as sound, to give an audio representation of the scatter trend. The sounds will play from left to right on the X axis, starting with the oldest marathon winning times and ending with the most recent. The pitch of the sounds decreases as the winning times go down. The male and female plots will play after each other.
